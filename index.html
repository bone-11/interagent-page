InterAgent
Multi-Agent Reasoning for Human-Object-Interaction Video Generation via Implicit Representation Alignment

Abstract

Recent advances in video diffusion models have spurred interest in human–object interaction (HOI) video generation, which demands fine-grained reasoning over interaction logic beyond single-subject animation. Existing methods rely heavily on explicit motion control, limiting scalability and generalization across diverse objects and interactions. We propose InterAgent, a thinking-before-generation framework for text-driven HOI video generation that bridges the gap between high-level intent and physical execution through explicit multi-agent reasoning over perception, interaction, and motion planning. To further enhance interaction modeling, we introduce an implicit text–motion alignment strategy that distills text-to-motion priors into the video diffusion model, enabling robust HOI synthesis without explicit motion inputs at inference. Experiments show that InterAgent significantly improves interaction naturalness, object appearance preservation, and adherence to complex textual instructions across challenging object-centric scenarios such as wearing and riding.

Method Framework

InterAgent consists of two core components: agent-based HOI reasoning and motion-free HOI video generation. The former employs a multi-agent architecture to infer structured and physically plausible HOI action plans from textual and image inputs, while the latter enhances text controllability by aligning video representations with implicit text-to-motion features during training, enabling robust text-driven HOI synthesis without explicit motion inputs at inference time.

Comparisons with SOTAs

We demonstrate the controllability of our method across a variety of weak human pose scenarios. The results show that our framework effectively adapts to diverse poses while maintaining coherent object interactions and consistent appearance.

Influence of Agents

We explore the impact of combining weak pose guidance with text-based control. The results demonstrate that our method can effectively integrate minimal pose information with textual semantics to generate coherent and semantically aligned motions.
